Race and Housing Discrimination

Mortgage Discrimination

 

There is evidence of mortgage discrimination occurring against blacks who have identical credit profiles, in terms of rejecting more profiles and having higher mortgage interest rates. Evidence as shown later suggests that this is due to statistical discrimination.

 

Racial Discrimination in the U.S. Housing and Mortgage Lending Markets: A Quantitative Review of Trends, 1976–2016

  • Looks at time series results from 16 field experiments of housing discrimination and 19 observational studies of mortgage lending discrimination
  • Finds evidence of a decline in housing discrimination from the late 1970s to the present
  • The decline is strongest for discrimination involving direct denial of housing availability, and weaker for measures reflecting the number of units recommended and inspected, and significant discrimination remains for these outcomes.
  • Racial gaps in loan denial have declined only slightly, and racial gaps in mortgage cost (interest rate) have not declined at all, suggesting persistent discrimination

What's in a Picture? Evidence of Discrimination from Prosper.com

  • Finds that loan listings with blacks in the attached picture are 25-35% less likely than whites with similar credit profiles

Racial discrepancy in mortgage interest rates

  • “Using data from three waves of the U.S. Survey of Consumer Finance, our results suggest that black borrowers on average pay about 29 basis points more than comparable white borrowers. We also find that rate disparity mainly occurs to young borrowers with low education as well as those borrowers whose income and credit disqualify them for prime lending rates. Furthermore, among borrowers in the higher rate groups, black women seem to receive much more disparate treatment than black men. We conclude that, while the racial disparity in mortgage rates is widespread between black and white borrowers, it is the more financially vulnerable black women who suffer the most.”

Discrimination in mortgage lending

Blacks and Hispanics face extra challenges in getting home loans

 

Even controlling for credit score, black people are more likely to default on their mortgage, meaning it makes sense to accept fewer applicants.

 

Race, redlining, and residential mortgage loan performance

  • “In terms of the race or ethnic background of the borrower, results indicate a higher likelihood of default on the part of black households (compared to white households), while Hispanic, Asian, and American Indian or Alaskan native households are, for the most part, statistically indistinguishable from white borrowers with respect to default risk.”

Report to the Congress on Credit Scoring and Its Effects on the Availability and Affordability of Credit

  • “Consistently, across all three credit scores and all five performance measures, blacks, single individuals, individuals residing in lower-income or predominantly minority census tracts show consistently higher incidences of bad performance than would be predicted by the credit scores.”
  • “The data also track the performance difference for each loan category by creditscore group. In almost every category, blacks show a higher incidence of default than non-Hispanic white borrowers, although differences are, in some cases, small. However, two product areas, auto loans from finance companies and credit card loans, show consistently higher and larger default rates for blacks than for non-Hispanic white borrowers for all credit-score quintiles.”

 

As for mortgage costs, these completely go away when accounting for differences in discount points, which black borrowers pay fewer of. On net, mortgage costs appear to be identical.

 

Do Minorities Pay More for Mortgages?

  • Finds gaps by race and ethnicity in interest rates which are offset by differences in discount points
  • Black and hispanic borrowers pay fewer discount points
  • Black and hispanic borrowers face a point rate schedule equal to that of white borrowers, although they sort to different locations on that schedule
  • “In other words, lenders compensate minority and white borrowers equally when they select a higher interest rate, across the entire range of interest rates.”
  • There were no differences in total fees by race or ethnicity

 

Further evidence that whatever discrimination occurs is statistical discrimination comes from the fact that profits are equal or less from black borrowers compared to white borrowers, suggesting that black people are not being systematically disadvantaged from the lending policy.

 

What's in a Picture? Evidence of Discrimination from Prosper.com

  • While loan discrimination occurs against blacks, lenders still earn a lower net return for loans to blacks compared to loans to whites

Do Minorities Pay More for Mortgages?

  • Lenders were found to earn slightly higher revenue and higher profit on mortgages from minorities people, but not because the lenders charged higher prices to minority borrowers but because they are able to extract higher margins from borrowers regardless of race and ethnicity who want lower upfront costs.

Rental Discrimination

 

There is also discrimination in the rental housing market, although it appears that much of it is due to statistical discrimination as well.

 

Closed doors everywhere? A meta-analysis of field experiments on ethnic discrimination in rental housing markets

  • “this article provides a quantitative meta-analysis of field experiments (in person audits and correspondence tests) that were run over the last four decades in the United States, Canada and Europe (N = 71). Special focus is given to a possible inflation of effect sizes by publication bias; to time trends; and to evidence for statistical discrimination. Taken together, nearly all experiments document the occurrence of ethnic discrimination. Effect sizes are inflated by publication bias, but there is still substantial evidence left once the bias is removed. The analysis reveals a consistent decline in the extent of discrimination over time, from moderate levels of discrimination in the 1970s and 1980s, up to only small but still statistically significant levels in the 1990s and 2000s. A significant part of the discriminatory behaviour can be attributed to missing information about the social status of applicants, which supports theories on statistical discrimination.”

 

Experimental research does show that race affects a person’s ability to view apartments, independent of voucher status.

 

Qualified Renters Need Not Apply Race and Voucher Discrimination in the Metro Boston Rental Housing Market

  • Looks at how race and voucher status affects people’s ability to view apartments
  • Finds that substantial discrimination exists, which primarily negatively affects black people
