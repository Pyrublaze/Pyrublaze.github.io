Global Warming Scientific Consensus

 

There is an overwhelming scientific and organizational consensus affirming the legitimacy of the anthropogenic global warming thesis, the idea that the temperature of the earth is increasing and that this is heavily caused by humans.

 

Scientific Consensus: Earth's Climate Is Warming

  • Provides a list of 18 scientific organizations which state that climate change is occurring.

List of Worldwide Scientific Organizations

  • Longer list of scientific organizations which believe in the concept of anthropogenic climate change.

Expert credibility in climate change

  • Examines the publication and citation data of 1372 climate researchers
  • Finds a 97-98% consensus on the tenets of anthropogenic climate change among climate scientists who most actively publish in the field.
  • Those who are skeptical of the tenets tend not to be as active in publishing.

Quantifying the consensus on anthropogenic global warming in the scientific literature

  • Looks at 11,944 climate abstracts
  • Finds a 97% consensus on anthropogenic global warming among the papers that stated a position.
  • Using a sample of the scientists who sampled their papers, they also got a 97% consensus.

Scientists Reach 100% Consensus on Anthropogenic Global Warming

  • Finds that as of 2019 using a sample of over 11,000 papers that there is a 100% consensus on anthropogenic global warming.

IPCC reflects scientific consensus on climate change

  • The IPCC doesn’t conduct research, but synthesizes research from hundreds of scientists, peer reviewed if necessary.
  • There is no major scientific body, such as the National Academy of Sciences (US), which rejects the conclusions of the IPCC.

The Cook et al. (2013) 97% consensus result is robust

  • The Cook et al. 2013 result has been replicated in many other studies, using both assessments of abstracts and author self-ratings.
  • Criticisms of it use erroneous arguments.
  • For example, one paper which argues that the 97% result is actually a 0.3% result only looks at the percentage of examined climatology papers which quantify human-caused global warming that affirms anthropogenic global warming. Moreover, extending this methodology, only 0.08% of papers would reject anthropogenic global warming.
