Economic Freedom

Economic freedom is overwhelmingly associated with positive outcomes, although there is often variation in outcomes when specific aspects of economic freedom are pulled apart. Notable results are that economic freedom is good for economic growth, increasing quality of life and happiness, increasing political freedom and tolerance, reducing unemployment and increasing innovation. This suggests that capitalism, which promotes economic freedom through things such as property rights, is a superior economic model.

General

 

Aggregate analysis of studies of economic freedom suggests that economic freedom is overwhelmingly associated with positive outcomes, although may be associated with greater inequality.

 

ECONOMIC FREEDOM OF THE WORLD: AN ACCOUNTING OF THE LITERATURE

Systematic literature review

  • Finds that in the vast majority of studies which cite the economic freedom index, it is associated with mostly good outcomes.
  • Some of the negative studies suggested that economic freedom increases income inequality

Economic Growth

 

Economic freedom increases economic growth.

 

Economic Freedom, Political Freedom, and Economic Well-Being: A Causality Analysis

  • Granger causality study
  • Finds that economic freedom causes the level of economic well-being for both industrial and nonindustrial countries, and that the level of economic well-being causes economic freedom, suggesting a feedback loop
  • No evidence was found of a relationship between economic freedom or political freedom

Economic freedom and economic growth: Does specification make a difference?

  • Meta-analysis
  • Finds a positive effect of economic freedom on economic growth

Components of Economic Freedom and Growth: An Empirical Study

  • Economic freedom is positively related to economic growth, through a number of channels, which include low money growth rate, small role played by government enterprises, rare negative real interest rates, small difference between the official and black-market exchange rates, large size of the trade sector, and freedom of citizens to engage in capital transactions with foreigners

Economic freedom, economic policy, and the tradeoff between equity and economic growth

  • Finds that economic freedom promotes both economic growth and equity, although there is a small tradeoff between economic growth and equity

Poverty and Inequality

 

Studies on inequality go both ways as for whether economic freedom increases or decreases it. Given the well-known tradeoff between equity and efficiency that exists and given that research overwhelmingly shows that economic freedom increases economic growth and thus efficiency, it’s fair to suggest economic freedom likely increases income inequality. Aspects of economic freedom appear to be associated with poverty reduction.

 

Economic freedom and income inequality: further evidence from 58 countries in the long-run

  • Panel study of 58 countries from 1980-2010
  • Finds that economic freedom overall reduces income inequality, although the relationship is not perfectly linear

ECONOMIC FREEDOM AND INCOME INEQUALITY REVISITED: EVIDENCE FROM A PANEL ERROR CORRECTION MODEL

  • Finds that lower economic freedom increases income inequality, and this is causal in both directions.
  • This may mean that some states could potentially get caught in cycles of high income inequality and heavy income redistribution

Economic Freedom and Income Inequality: Does Political Regime Matter?

  • Finds that economic freedom increases income inequality, although this is attenuated with democratic regimes.

An Inquiry into the Relationship Between Economic Freedom and Social Mobility

  • Finds that economic freedom increases social mobility

An Empirical Note on Economic Freedom and Income Inequality

  • Economic freedom has a positive but relatively inelastic effect on income inequality

Poverty and Economic Freedom: Evidence from Cross-Country Data

  • Looks at economic freedom and poverty for 40 developing countries
  • Finds that important indicators of economic freedom such as openness to trade and small government are associated with poverty reduction.
  • Civil liberties which encompass aspects of economic freedom such as rule of law and property rights are also associated with poverty reduction

Quality of Life and Happiness

 

Economic freedom increases quality of life and self-reported happiness and well-being.

 

Economic Freedom and Quality of Life: Evidence from the OECD’s Your Better Life Index

  • Finds that economic freedom is significantly positively associated with a multitude of indicators of quality of life

Private Property Rights, Economic Freedom, and Well Being

  • Finds that countries with more economic freedom are wealthier and have longer life expectancies, in addition to countries that encroach less on the private property rights of their citizens

Freedom and Happiness: A comparative study in 46 nations in the early 1990's

  • Looks at 46 nations in the early 1990s
  • Finds that the opportunity to choose and the capability to choose in nations is positively related to happiness among rich nations, but not among poor nations
  • Opportunity for free trade is positively related to happiness in poor nations, but not in rich nations

Who Benefits from Economic Freedom? Unraveling the Effect of Economic Freedom on Subjective Well-Being

  • Looks at 86 countries from 1990-2005
  • Finds that overall economic freedom has a significant positive effect on subjective well-being
  • Its dimensions legal security and property rights, sound money, and regulation are in particular strong predictors of higher well-being
  • Culture moderates the effect, and societies that are more tolerant and have a positive attitude toward the market economy profit the most

Is Economic Freedom the Secret to Happiness?

  • Finds that economic freedom, which coincides with democracy and economic growth, positively affects happiness
  • Economic freedom refers to the ability of individuals to use their property as they see fit

Economic Freedom and Happiness

  • Looks at over 100 countries
  • Finds a positive relationship between national levels of happiness and economic freedom
  • GDP per capita also exerts a strong positive influence on happiness, and GDP per capita and economic freedom appear to be correlated with one another

Happiness and economic freedom: Are they related?

  • Finds that rule of law, regulatory efficiency, and open markets positively affect subjective well-being
  • There is a negative relationship between limited government and subjective well-being, possibly due to it causing lower expenditures and higher unemployment

Free to Be Happy: Economic Freedom and Happiness in US States

  • Finds that economic freedom increases happiness in US states

Economic Freedom and the Quality of Life: An Empirical Analysis

  • Economic freedom increases quality of life

Economic Freedom, Democracy, and the Quality of Life

  • Economic freedom consistently increases health, education, and disease prevention in society.

Economic Freedom and Quality of Life Impacts: A Comparison

  • Even though countries with more economic freedom have higher inequality, it is balanced out by an overall higher quality of life

Political Freedom and Tolerance

 

Economic freedom coincides with greater political freedom and greater tolerance for others.

 

“Economic freedom and democracy: determinant factors in increasing macroeconomic stability”

  • Looks at 11 countries of the EU
  • Finds that economic freedom is positively associated with democracy and macroeconomic stability

Examining the Hayek–Friedman hypothesis on economic and political freedom

  • Looks at 123 nations as far back as 1970
  • Findfs relatively few instances of societies combining relatively high political freedom without relatively high levels of economic freedom

Does Economic Freedom Foster Tolerance?.

  • Study of up to 65 countries
  • Finds that economic freedom is positively related to tolerance towards homosexuals, especially in the longer run, while tolerance towards people of a different race and a willingness to teach kids tolerance are not strongly affected by how free markets are

Economic Freedom as a Driver of Trust and Tolerance

  • Finds that economic freedom overall increases tolerance of different groups of people, notably homosexuals and those of other races
  • The relationship between economic freedom and antisemitism is more complex

ECONOMIC FREEDOM AND CONFLICT REDUCTION: EVIDENCE FROM THE 1970S, 1980S, AND 1990S

  • Finds that economic freedom is strongly connected with conflict reduction

Air Quality

 

Economic freedom can decrease local air pollution but does not appear to reduce more global scale emissions such as carbon dioxide.

 

Economic Freedom and Air Quality

  • Finds that economic freedom appears to create incentives to abate local air pollution such as fine particulate matter, although this does not appear to be the case for carbon dioxide emissions which are more global in nature.

Innovation

 

Economic freedom appears to increase corporate innovation and some aspects increase innovation in general.

 

Corporate Innovation and Economic Freedom: Cross-Country Comparisons

  • Looks at 5809 firms from 29 countries over the 1984-2006 period
  • Finds strong and robust evidence of a positive relationship between corporate innovation and cross-country differences in economic freedom

Economic Freedom and Innovation

  • Looks at data from 139 countries
  • Finds that property rights, freedom from corruption, fiscal freedom, business freedom, trade freedom, investment freedom, and financial freedom all influence a country’s innovation level
  • Property rights, freedom from corruption, and business freedom are positively associated with innovation, while fiscal freedom and investment freedom are negatively associated with innovation

Labor Effects

 

Economic freedom is associated with lower unemployment, among other positive labor market effects.

 

Economic Freedom and Unemployment around the World

THE IMPACT OF ECONOMIC FREEDOM ON THE UNEMPLOYMENT RATE IN O.E.C.D. NATIONS: AN EXPLORATORY STUDY ACCEPTING THE VALIDITY OF OKUN’S LAW

  • Higher economic freedom reduces unemployment

ECONOMIC FREEDOM AND LABOR MARKET CONDITIONS: EVIDENCE FROM THE STATES

  • Uses 1981-1989 data for 50 states
  • Finds that economic freedom is associated with lower unemployment and higher labor force participation and employment-population ratios

Other

 

Economic freedom is associated with less debt risk and less governmental corruption.

 

Economic freedom and sovereign credit ratings and default risk

  • Finds that after controlling for factors such as per capita GDP, growth and political regime, lower economic freedom results in a significantly higher cost of borrowing for a country

Does Economic Freedom Influence Governmental Corruption? An Empirical Analysis

  • Finds a strong correlation and statistically significant relationship between economic freedom and governmental corruption, with higher economic freedom being associated with lower governmental corruption
