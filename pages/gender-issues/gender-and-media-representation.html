Media Representation

 

The representation of men in the media tends to be negative compared to the representation of women, with men being portrayed as more violent and aggressive. Violence against men is seen as more normal than violence against women, and men with mental illnesses are more stigmatized than women with mental illnesses.

 

Men in the Media Today — A Contemporary Study

  • Men in the media today are overwhelmingly portrayed as negative, being frequently associated with violence and aggression.
  • With the exception of a small minority of positive media portrayals of male heroes such as war veterans, firefighters, and rescuers, and portrayals of men as good fathers, husbands, and citizens, the only males presented positively are men and boys who have been feminized.

Comparing gendered and generic representations of mental illness in Canadian newspapers: an exploration of the chivalry hypothesis

  • Finds that articles depicting men with mental illnesses are significantly more likely to have stigmatizing content and violence as themes
  • Articles depicting women are significantly more likely to quote mental health experts, discuss mental health interventions, and have recovery and inadequate resources as themes.

Reporting Violence in the British Print Media: Gendered Stories

  • Looks at how violence is reported in the British print media
  • Finds that women’s violence was more likely to be reported as irrational or emotional, with real wickedness ascribed in a few high-profile cases, while men’s violence was more likely to be presented as normal or rational.
