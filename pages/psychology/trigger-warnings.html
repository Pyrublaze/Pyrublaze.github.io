Trigger Warnings

 

Trigger warnings have no net effect on affective responses to material being warned, especially in the long run, and do not turn the vast majority of people away from such material. It can in fact increase engagement with material sometimes. Additionally, trigger warnings reinforce trauma survivor’s views of their trauma being central to their identity and increase anticipatory stress. This suggests that trigger warnings at best do essentially nothing and at worst have small negative effects.

 

Helping or Harming? The Effect of Trigger Warnings on Individuals With Trauma Histories

  • Finds that trigger warnings have no helpful effect on survivors of trauma and actually counter-therapeutically reinforce survivors’ views of their trauma being central to their identity.

A Meta-Analysis of the Effects of Trigger Warnings, Content Warnings, and Content Notes

  • Meta-analysis
  • Finds that warnings have no effect on affective responses to educational material or on educational outcomes (comprehension)
  • Warnings increased anticipatory effect
  • Effects on avoidance were mixed, suggesting they either have no effect or increase engagement with negative material under specific circumstances.

Student reactions to traumatic material in literature: Implications for trigger warnings

  • The vast majority of students tend to read triggering passages following such warnings
  • Of those who read it, those with triggering traumas did not report more distress although those with PTSD did
  • No one had an increase in trauma symptoms as a result of reading the triggering passage
